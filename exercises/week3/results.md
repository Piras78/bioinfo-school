## Surprises

List at least one model output that was hard to interpret and one validation habit you will reuse.

# Model output that was hard to interpret
The three-dimensional variations in the low-pLDDT tails in AlphaFold generated by changing the seed. At first glance, obtaining different spatial positions from the exact same sequence might seem like a precision error or a model hallucination. This output becomes interpretable only when the confidence score is combined with the biological concept of an “intrinsically disordered region”: the model is not making a mistake, but is describing the actual physical instability of that portion of the protein.

# Validation habit I will reuse
Always supplement the visual inspection of clusters with a quantitative and statistical test. Generating a visual map (such as UMAP or PCA) for the embeddings is useful for initial exploration, but it is essential to validate it mathematically by calculating vector distances (such as cosine similarity) and applying formal tests. This approach ensures that the groupings are objectively meaningful and not merely artifacts of dimensionality reduction.
